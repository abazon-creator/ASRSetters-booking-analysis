Booking a call comes down to one behaviour: naming a specific day and time.

48% of booked calls contain the agent naming an actual day and clock time ("Monday at 3 o'clock", "11:20 or 11:40"). In declined calls that figure is 3%. No other measured behaviour comes close to this gap — not empathy, not product knowledge, not call length. Agents who lose the booking overwhelmingly make a vague, open-ended offer ("just let me know if you'd like to set that up") and accept the first no.

Read this carefully. Empathy and entitlement framing ("this came with your purchase") appear in roughly equal measure in both groups — declined calls actually score slightly higher on empathy. Being warm is not the differentiator, and coaching agents to be warmer will not move the number. Asking for a specific slot is.

Method. Every connected call (talk time ≥ 90s) in the Aloware export for 14–21 Jul 2026 was parsed into agent and customer turns. Objections were detected from the customer's own words; a call counts as "closed after the objection" only when the agent asked for a booking in a turn that came after the customer's last objection. Coaching calls are declined calls where an objection was raised and the agent never asked again. Model calls are booked calls where an objection was raised and the agent asked again and won.

A caveat on the inbound calls. Many declined inbound calls are billing or app-support calls where the onboarding offer is bolted on after 20–50 minutes of troubleshooting, at the point the customer most wants to hang up. Those agents are not failing to close so much as being asked to close at the worst possible moment. That is a process problem, not a skill problem, and it is worth fixing before coaching the individuals.

On the Aloware QA score. 1,797 of these calls carry an automated rubric score. It does not predict booking: the four agents who clear the 40% target average 6.1/10, while several agents scoring above 7.0 sit under 25% booking. The rubric measures customer-care quality, which is a different job from setting. Do not use it to rank setters.
