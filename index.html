One behaviour predicts the booking better than everything else combined.

Naming a specific day and clock time — "Monday at 3 o'clock", "11:20 or 11:40" — appears in 48% of booked calls and 3% of declined ones. Nothing else comes close. Empathy and value explanation appear slightly more often in the calls we lose. The close is already written into the script; it simply isn't being used.

What the analysis found

Four things that change what to do next. Each is worked through in the reports above.

29.7%

The reported booking rate is wrong

The Closer Report shows 43.56% for July. Measured consistently over the same window it is 29.7%. The 90 sec Calls denominator stops updating while sets keep accruing — which is also what produces weekly rates above 100%. Worth fixing at source before anyone makes decisions on that number.

0 of 27

Inbound and outbound are different businesses

Outbound connects on 4.9% of dials but books 36.4% of those. Inbound connects on 58.6% and books 25.3%. Teams are organised by channel, so the team league table is largely a channel ranking. No inbound agent reaches 40% — a single blended target manufactures failure for three quarters of the floor.

43%

"Not interested" isn't what it sounds like

It is 43% of every objection and we lose 92% of them. But members keep saying "all I wanted was the golf clubs", "I don't want any instruction", "I don't want any videos". They are refusing a sales pitch we never made. The rebuttal has to kill that belief before anything else.

6.1/10

The QA score doesn't predict booking

Calls carry an automated rubric score. The four agents hitting target average 6.1; several scoring above 7.0 book under 25%. The rubric measures customer-care quality, which is a different job from setting. It should not be used to rank setters.

Method. Appointments are counted as distinct member email addresses per agent per day from the Closer Report, rather than counting form rows — roughly 5% of rows are repeat bookings of an address already booked that month. Connected calls are those with talk time of 90 seconds or more. Transcripts were split into rep and member turns; objections come from the member's own wording, and a call counts as "asked again" only when the rep requested a booking after the member's last objection.

Limits. 13 July is missing from the call export — it was the busiest day of the period and the file exceeded the download limit. Two teams show appointments but no call records, so no rate is computed for them. Weekly and monthly views are partial, since the call window is eight days. Agents with fewer than 30 connected calls are dimmed in the agent table: at that sample the confidence band is wide enough to mislead.
