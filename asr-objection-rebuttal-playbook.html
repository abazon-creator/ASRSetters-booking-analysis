"Not interested" is 43% of every objection we hear — and we lose 92% of them.

But read what members actually say after those two words. "All I wanted was the golf clubs." "I don't want any instruction, I don't want any videos." "I went through this with somebody the last time about doing all the classes." "I'll call you, don't call me."

They are not declining a free setup call. They are declining what they think you are about to sell them. The member believes this is another pitch, or a course they're being enrolled in. Every rebuttal below starts by killing that belief before anything else — because if they still think you're selling, nothing else you say lands.

Five rules that apply to every objection

Measured across all 1,347 booked and declined calls.

How these were built. Every connected call (talk time ≥ 90 seconds) between 14–21 July 2026 was parsed into rep and member turns. Wherever the rep asked for the appointment, the member's reply was captured and classified from their own wording. 1,949 calls contained a genuine ask; 860 of them (44%) drew at least one objection. Rebuttals are modelled on what reps who won those same objections actually said — every proof call is linked.

What is already in the script and is not being used. The Appointment-Setting Script already says "I have [Date/Time] — which of those do you prefer?" Only 3% of declined calls contain a rep naming an actual day and clock time, against 48% of booked calls. The single biggest gain available is reps executing the close the script already tells them to use.

One honest caveat. On cancellation and refund calls the member is rarely winnable — 92% decline, and pushing risks the CSAT score. Process the refund properly, make one clean offer, and if the answer is no again, let them go.
